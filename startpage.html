Background

Early Life:
Louis Vuitton was bron on August 4, 1821, in Anchay, France. He came from a working-class family, his father was a farmer and his mother was a milliner. At 13, he left home and traveled to paris. He later became apprentice box-maker and packer for Monsieur Marchal in Paris. He was excellent at creating custom boxes and trunks for transporting personal belongings.
Accomplishments:
He founded the Louis Vuitton brand in 1854, originally specializing in high-quality trunks and luggage. In 1896 the iconic monogram was introduced by his son Georges to prevent counterfeiting and to solidify the brand. His clientele included French royalty, nobleman, and wealthy traveler, which helped promote the brands reputation.
